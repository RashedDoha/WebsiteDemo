BUET closed for indefinite period

Students have been instructed to leave residential halls by 5pm Thursday, said the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology student welfare wing chief Delwar Hossain. “The decision followed violence on the campus by students demanding to postpone exams,” he said. A notice was posted at the residential halls around 2:30pm.

Signed by BUET Registrar AKM Masud, it said that a group of students - demanding that the examinations be pushed back – held the vice-chancellor, registrar and student welfare director hostage on Tuesday. The demonstrators blocked the teachers’ residential area on Wednesday and later damaged and set fire to the academic building, the authorities said in the notice. “All forms of academic activities of the university have been suspended for an indefinite period in an effort to bring back discipline to the campus and ensure a proper atmosphere for education,” the notice read. The university, however, said ‘some of its students’ were demanding that the tests be postponed. “Term final examinations are usually held twice a week, but due to Ramadan only one was taken. But some students demonstrated to force a delay in the exams,” claimed BUET Student Welfare Wing Director Prof Delwar Hossain.
